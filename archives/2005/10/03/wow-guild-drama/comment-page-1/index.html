    I’ll try to recount it to you…

    OP is new to this guild, having been a member no more than a couple of days.

A fellow guildmate priest who was in the raid gets all pissy about it for some reason and quits the guild over it.

    OP puts the hide up in the AH. Afterwards, OP feels a little bit of remorse about it, and discusses the matter with a guild officer. By the OPs reckoning, he’s going to give the hide up to go in the guild bank, and get a lot of ZGP and DKP for it.

    He mails off the hide, but no reply concerning ZGP (which the OP couldn’t care less about) and DKP (which the OP _does_ want) is forthcoming. After asking the guild leader about it, he basically gets, “We didn’t authorize Officer #1 to give you any DKP or ZGP. Thanks for the hide.”

    At this point he’s understandably a little upset, so he goes public with it. The general consensus is, “Wow, you got ninja’d by your own guild. Count it as a life lesson learned, complain to a GM, and find another guild. And good luck with all that.”

    Word spreads from the general forum that he posted on, to the realm forum (Dunemaul). At this point, a couple of asshats from the guild (Order of the Sun) pretty much show their asses in the forum, and the consensus is the same as above with the addition of OotS getting a big fat black eye from it.

    At some point in the proceedings Blizz gets involved, but I never saw the actual message. This thing was 15+ pages on each thread (general and Dunemaul), so I could’ve easily overlooked it.

    Finally, some OotS officer high enough to do something about it (the founder I think) gets on, says that it’s all been a big misunderstanding, OotS is really a great bunch of guys who’d never ninja loot, the asshats are really wonderful people once you get to know them, and OP will be getting his hide back. Most posters think that this was done more to try to put out the flames rather than it being the Right Thing To Do, but are glad to see justice done in the end. OP drops a couple of hints that it might be a little more than a misunderstanding, but takes the high road and lets it drop. He’s getting his hide back, which is more than he expected.

    That was pretty much where I left it. The Dunemaul villagers were gathered around OotS with the torches, demanding the asshats’ heads, but nothing had come of that last I saw. Funny stuff, but it stops being funny when it starts being you.
