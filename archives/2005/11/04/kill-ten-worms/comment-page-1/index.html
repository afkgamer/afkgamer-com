Kill Ten Worms

Posted on November 4, 2005 by Foton

I am the worst quester in the entire universe of MMO worlds. People know this about me. I know this about myself. They work around it; I work around it.

I’ll put in a good 5 or 10 minutes of effort into figuring out a quest’s detailed objectives, then I’ve had enough of that and will google up a spoiler. If I’m no closer to knowing what the hell I have to do after 5 or 10 minutes, I figure wasting even more time isn’t going to solve the problem.

Obviously, I won’t ever be the first to solve an epic quest. I’m comfortable with that.

For example, the revamped Silithus zone in the much beloved World of Warcraft has several quest lines for the higher level player. Soon after the revamp, I rode out there. (And when I say “ride”, I mean I actually had to ride my damn horse there because they moved the griffin port so I had to learn the flight route all over again. Fun.) Hot damn, there were many quest NPCs and there was a strong possibility that one of them might be worth a damn.

One of the quest lines is for a cooking recipe. I know this because the quest log shows the quest under Cooking. (My mom was right. I am smart.) I also know that A. the tougher recipes usually give a nice temporary stat buff and B. these are usually easy quests. Yay and yay.

Here’s the quest text:

Look for information about cooking Sandworm Meat at the Twilight’s Hammer camp west of Cenarion Hold.

How simple is that. Quest “information” usually indicates it’s some paper or it’s in a crate, maybe it’s an NPC you have to hail and such. (Well, the Warcraft equivalent of hail.) Since Cenarion Hold is the Silithus outpost with the griff port, this couldn’t be much easier.

If I was a different person, it couldn’t be much easier.

I do a slow loop of the zone, stop off at the elementals to farm two or three Essence of Airs (ka-ching, ka-ching), and next door is a Twilight Hammer camp, sure enough, almost directly west of Cenarion Hold. I have to kill some of the Twilight Hammer for a different quest, which I do (ka-ching, ka-ching), and life is good.

Soon, all I have left is this THEORETICALLY easy cooking quest.

Foton thinks: Hmm, maybe I missed one of the little camps here with the crate, I’ll check again.

And thinks: Hmm, maybe one of these bozos out here took it already and I have to wait for repop.

And thinks: This is definitely west of that town.

And thinks: If I was an NPC assigned to watch that shithole of a town, I’d be pissed. Wonder who they ticked off to get that crap assignment.

And thinks: If I was a designer, I’d name NPCs after people I hate and assign them to a shithole like that.

And thinks: Goddamit, if this is a repop thing … (trails off into thoughts of fire and destruction)

Asks the guild: Anybody done this Sili-whatever cooking quest yet? *hears crickets chirping* (Note: I’m already looking for a spoiler. Think that was about 3 minutes this time.)

I alt-tab out to Thottbot to end the torture. In this situation, it’s isn’t very revealing. I recheck my position on the map.

Foton thinks: Ok, there’s that shithole town and here’s west of that shithole town. FFS. I’ve checked everyw … oh. Hmm. Wonder what’s that little building icon next to Shithold that is also directly west.

I wonder no more. It is also a Twilight Hammer camp … the CORRECT Twilight Hammer camp. I got my info, rode back to Shithold, quest completed. Racked up a new set of quests.

Foton reads: Make 10 worm nutballs and return to Annoying NPC for a reward of questionable value.

And thinks: Where the fuck am I going to get worm nuts? Worms have nuts? Ok, now I wish I didn’t have that mental image.

Alt-tab.
