We Need More Fluffers

Posted on January 13, 2005 by Foton

In my neverending quest to assume secret identities, I become the undead.

This was part of a quest reward I completed recently: a drink with 5 charges that granted various illusions for ten minutes each. Other than the occasional uber equipment reward (read: extremely occasional), this reward was our favorite.

Our group (it’s not a party, dammit!) drank up and started to put the hurt on some dinos: one guy was 2 feet tall, the remaining four were skellies. This Horde rogue wandered past and did a virtual double-take. He passed, he stopped, he turned around and stared.

Through the magic of /em’s, he told us that he had been trying to say hello and ‘sup for the past few minutes until it dawned on him that all was not as it appeared. He seemed like a nice enough guy, for a Horde, even with the flesh dripping off his skeleton, so we allowed him to pass through Our Zone unmolested.

I came to a realization that some of the best stuff in MMOGs is the nonsense. Not like nerf nonsense, but fun nonsense … the meaningless fluff. The goofy proc’ing weapons, the illusions, the animated emotes, the weapon graphics: everyone wants them and will pay top gold to have them.

I don’t want a game with nothing but fluff, but sometimes, in between the grinds, a little fluff is just what I need. I don’t know if game houses have a guy in charge of the fluff, but I think they should. (His title shouldn’t be The Fluffer though — that shit could cause serious psychological damage.)

Remember EQ’s Mask of Deception? The dark elf illusion mask that was nerfed to kingdom come because trolls and ogres could wantonly shrink at will? (GOD NO! Not wanton shrinkage!) It became the stuff of legends and it really wasn’t that useful — wanton shrinking aside.

I suspect, and don’t let this get around, but I suspect that people would pay real money just for game fluff. (Oh wait, they already have — recall many of the EQ expansions)

Right now, I’d pay 5 or 10 bucks just to have another 5 shots of that illusion drink.
