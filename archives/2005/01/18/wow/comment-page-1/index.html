Wow.

Posted on January 18, 2005 by Foton

This past weekend in the Warcrap world can be summed up in a word: catastrophe. Although any word that means “fucked up big” would do. (Blizzard’s most recent apology)

Some background: I don’t have queues on my server yet. But, performance has been dwindling rather steadily as of late, so it’s probably only a matter of days before the queues arrive. My first Warcrap character was on one of the large PVP servers, and after a few weeks and 20 levels, I had enough of the multi-hour queues and fled to my current server, which had a population of roughly 12 people.

At first, the low population was kinda the suck, since nothing was ever on the AH and there weren’t many players to do larger instances, etc., but as other refugees arrived, the situation improved … and went beyond improved back to sucking again.

My server’s woes are nothing compared to those other servers, however.

This should be interesting to watch (from very, very far away), as this is not only a public relations disaster for our Blizzard overlords, but potentially a financial disaster as well. They really can’t take people’s money for a box then say, “Bummer, we can’t deliver the service. Thanks for stopping, man.” I mean, they can try, and it certainly seems that they are, but in the end, that won’t work.

Now, what I find particularly humorous about the situation is: which part of “we’ve just sold 600,000 boxes” did they not understand? After the queues in Open Beta, even I (fool that I am) figured I better get my gaming ass to a store the first day before they limit box sales to ensure adequate service. In hindsight, I was an idiot — they didn’t become alarmed until much later.

At my office, we do project work. We don’t keep taking on every project that’s tossed our way, hoping that we’ll have enough people to complete the jobs. We regularly turn away work because promising things we can’t deliver WOULD BE BAD. We know, because of COMMON SENSE, that we can’t hire 100 people each week and get them up to speed in a few days. Weeks later, the work noobs are still asking about office equipment and expense reports. (No, we won’t pay for a round of golf on the road, ffs.)

I haven’t decided yet if the Blizzard Collective are just raw noobs or if they’re fucking arrogant raw noobs. I’m beginning to suspect the latter.

I got news for them: this game of yours, it’s good. But it ain’t that good.
