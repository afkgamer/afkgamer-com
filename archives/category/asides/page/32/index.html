O NOES, FOES

Posted on May 5, 2006 by Foton
3

An article in the BBC News today, “Problems plague World of Warcraft” got me to chuckling this morning. It’s about the server problems as of late (i.e. the past 18 months) and Blizzard’s recent rah-rah State of the Game address. They talk to a few players who have respectable real lives — not slobs like you and me — and, in particular, they spoke with Jeff Woleslagle, an editor at Ten Ton Hammer. Let me clarify that I like Ten Ton Hammer, I don’t think they’re idiots; it’s a little too ad-y for my tastes, but we all have to pay the bills. At any rate, teh BBC paraphrased Mr. Woleslagle’s comments on how lag can affect gameplay, “These tiny delays often occured at the most inconvenient times. For instance, they could mean that healing spells were not cast in time and player-controlled characters got overwhelmed by foes.” This is how immature I am, I think that’s hilarious. Foes. OH NOES, We’re overwhelmed by FOES! Maybe they talk like that on the RP servers, but on the regular servers for slobs like us, fancy boys and their foes are not welcome. (God. I’m so easily amused.)

Innervate Please

Posted on May 4, 2006 by Foton
13

Good news, Warcraft Druids. Innervate, the highly desired druid talent to infuse raid priests with hyper mana regeneration, will be moved to a base ability, available at level 40. Eyonix, Blizzard’s flying whelp, posted that Patch 1.11 will include the change to innervate as well as a new 31-point talent. Nightshroud of the Alleria realm, new enemy of raid priests everywhere, posted: “Excellent. More freedom for Druids to spec how they please and not how the Priests please.” (Because we all know that priests get to spec however they please. Even I know that’s not true.) Here’s a fun trick to play on your guild priests, by the way. Ask them at the next raid how come they’re not shadow spec’d, you heard that spec was wtfpwnage. You should get quite a rise out of them. (Furl archive of Patch 1.11 Innervate announcement)
