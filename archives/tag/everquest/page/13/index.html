Dumb Luck That

Posted on March 9, 2005 by Foton
1

A few of us in the e-thug guild came from the same EverQuest server and the same guild — eventually that is, after brief vacations in other virtual worlds. We are quite the melting pot of online gaming if you were to examine the myriad relationships and histories that brought us altogether into World of Warcraft. The current clique works and that’s all that matters at this point.

But the few of us, we came from EverQuest, same guild. And on our former EQ server, we had arch enemies, as was natural for anyone that played EQ for longer than six months — after about six months, more or less, we all had a list of players that had fucked us over repeatedly, and we, them, in turn.

It was a tight community … like a noose.

Games as Portals

Posted on March 2, 2005 by Foton
5

The game is afoot.

Everybody’s making fun of /pizza. Leno’s using it in his monologue (a welcome change from his usual Ovaltine-esque bits); CNN is on board; the news has even spread across the big puddle to the UK’s Register (took a while for that).

Today’s Google news search — 105 hits.

It’s such a goddamn goofy idea. Not well implemented, and so damn goofy, but it’s only the beginning of Online Games as Portals.

Old game hags may remember EverQuest’s Instant Messenger: Offline players could message other EQIM users who were either in game or out — there was even a Trillian plugin for EQIM. I’m not sure if other online games offered such a thing, but every game I’ve played after that, players have asked if similar offline chatting was available.
